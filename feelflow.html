01 — Overview

What is FeelFlow?

FeelFlow is a mood-journaling app designed for people who have lower emotional self-awareness. The idea specifically targets children with Autism Spectrum Disorder, ADHD, and other emotional disorders. The website check in with yourself in under two minutes, every day to track your emotional progress.

This project was co-organized with my father, using AI to program what we designed. I designed the UX/UI furthr in detail after the base draft.

The Problem

Existing mood-tracking apps are either too clinical and hard to habitualize. Children are especially a harder target audience to keep a regular data input.

The Goal

Design a low-friction journaling experience that feels gentle and personal — one that encourages daily habit formation through calm visuals, minimal input, and meaningful reflection.

02 — Define

Key Design Principles

I defined three guiding principles for FeelFlow before moving into ideation:

01

Gentle, not gamified

No streaks, no scores. The app rewards reflection, not consistency metrics.

02

Quick process

Every core interaction must be completable in under 2 minutes to reduce friction.

03

Your words, your pace

Free-form journaling alongside structured prompts — users choose how much to share.

04 — Design

Key Screens

The design uses a soft, muted palette with large touch targets and generous spacing. Mood selection uses illustrated faces rather than numerical scales to feel less clinical.

Design Decisions

  • Illustrated mood icons instead of sliders
  • Soft green & warm cream palette
  • One-tap daily check-in from home screen widget
  • Optional journal prompt to reduce blank-page anxiety
  • Swipe-based navigation to avoid deep menu trees
  • Weekly mood summary as a gentle visual recap
